About Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels in the retina (the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye).

At first, diabetic retinopathy may cause no symptoms or only mild vision problems. Eventually, it can cause blindness.

Stages of Diabetic Retinopathy:

  • No Diabetic Retinopathy - No abnormalities detected
  • Mild Non-Proliferative DR - Small areas of balloon-like swelling in the retina's tiny blood vessels
  • Moderate Non-Proliferative DR - More blockages in blood vessels of the retina
  • Severe Non-Proliferative DR - Many more blood vessels are blocked, depriving areas of the retina of blood supply
  • Proliferative DR - Most advanced stage, new abnormal blood vessels grow in the retina
